Historical Places in India

Red Fort

The Red Fort, situated in the heart of Delhi, India, is a historical attraction and a symbol of the city's rich history and culture.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb is a tomb in Delhi, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Humayun in 1569, and is the first example of Mughal garden tomb architecture in India.

Hampi

Located in Karnataka, Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the site of the once-glorious Vijayanagara Empire. It features numerous temples, palaces, and ruins amidst a unique boulder-strewn landscape

Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar is a collection of astronomical instruments and a monument in Jaipur, India. It was constructed in 1728 by the Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the ruler of Jaipur.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a 72.5-metre (238 ft) tall historic minaret located in the Qutub complex in Delhi, India. It was built by the Islamic ruler Qutb-ud-din Aybak in 1199.

India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located astride the Rajpath, a ceremonial avenue in New Delhi, India. It was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and Sir Herbert Baker, and built to commemorate the British and Indian soldiers who fought in the First World War.

Raigad

Raigad is a historic fort located in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort stands atop a hill and offers stunning views of the Western Ghats. Raigad is a popular tourist destination and a symbol of Maratha valor and history.
